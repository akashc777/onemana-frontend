<h2 style="text-align: center;">The Story</h2>

<p>It all started with a simple question: <strong>"Why do I need 17 different apps just to work with my team?"</strong></p>

<p>Slack for chatting. Jira for tasks. Google Docs for writing. Zoom for meetings. Notion for notes. And a spreadsheet to track which app has what. It was getting ridiculous.</p>

<p>So instead of complaining about it on Twitter like a normal person, I decided to build <strong>OneCamp</strong> a self-hosted workspace that puts Chat, Tasks, Docs, and Meetings all in one place. Because apparently, I enjoy pain.</p>

<p>OneCamp is built by <strong>OneMana Solutions (OPC) Private Limited</strong>, a one-person company (yes, the "OPC" stands for One Person Company it's literally in the name). Headquartered in the tech capital of India <strong>Bangalore</strong>  where the coffee is strong, the traffic is stronger, and the startups are strongest.</p>


<p>We believe that small teams (and solo warriors 🥷) deserve the same powerful tools that big companies get  without the enterprise price tag, without the vendor lock-in, and without someone else owning your data.</p>
